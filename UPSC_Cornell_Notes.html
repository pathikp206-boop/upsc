T-01 Pattanam / Pattana — Ancient Port Towns
Key Questions

What does Pattanam mean?

Which site = Muziris? Who excavated it?

Name 3 literary sources mentioning Muziris.

Keywords Muziri Pattanam KCHR Periplus Periyar River Pliny
Pattanam ≠ military/religious site. It is a coastal/riverine commercial trade settlement.

Definition

Pattanam (Tamil) / Pattana (Sanskrit) = coastal or riverine commercial port town. The "-pattanam" suffix always signals a trading settlement with water access. Distinguished from Nagara (admin city), Nigama (market), Shreni (guild).

Archaeological Site — Pattanam, Kerala

Believed to be Muziris — the greatest Indo-Roman emporium. Located near the delta of the Periyar River, Ernakulam district.

  • Excavated since 2006 by KCHR (Kerala Council of Historical Research) under Muziris Heritage Project
  • 300,000+ artefacts spanning 3rd century BCE – 10th century CE
  • Finds: Mediterranean amphorae, West Asian ceramics, rouletted ware, glass beads, boat planks/ship timbers

Literary Sources

Sangam poetry (Akananuru, Purananuru) Periplus of Erythraean Sea — 1st C CE Pliny's Natural History — "gold drain"

Terminology Distinctions

TermMeaningExample
Pattanam / PattanaCoastal/riverine port-townMuziri Pattanam, Kaveri Pattanam
NagaraInland admin cityPataliputra
NigamaMarket/trading settlementMauryan period refs
ShreniTrade/craft guild (NOT a place)—
SUMMARYPattanam = coastal commercial port-town; Pattanam site (Kerala) = likely Muziris; excavated by KCHR since 2006; 3rd C BCE – 10th C CE; 300,000+ finds including Roman amphorae.
T-02 Mechanism of the Southwest Monsoon
Key Questions

What is the core driver of monsoon?

What role does ITCZ shift play? When does it occur?

When does monsoon onset in Kerala? Cover all India?

Name both monsoon branches and their impact areas.

Keywords ITCZ Coriolis Force Easterly Jet Thar Low
ITCZ shift timing confusion. Easterly (Somali) Jet is linked to onset burst — don't confuse with westerly jet.

Core Driver

Differential heating — land heats/cools faster than ocean (lower specific heat). This pressure gradient drives everything.

Four-Step Causal Chain

Mar – MayThar Desert & Deccan heat up; Tibetan Plateau acts as elevated heat engine → surface low deepens
May – JunITCZ shifts north to 20°–25°N (becomes Monsoon Trough); Westerly jet retreats north; Easterly (Somali) Jet forms at 15°N → onset burst
JunDeep thermal low in Thar (<1000 hPa) — acts as vacuum pulling oceanic air
Jun+SE trade winds cross equator → deflected right by Coriolis → become SW winds; enter as two branches

Two Branches

BranchPathKey Areas
Arabian SeaHits Western Ghats (orographic) → crosses DeccanMumbai/Goa/Konkan 250+ cm; Deccan rain shadow <50 cm
Bay of BengalHits NE India → moves up Gangetic PlainAssam, WB, Bihar, UP, Delhi

Key Dates

Kerala ~1 Jun Mumbai ~10 Jun Delhi ~29 Jun All India ~15 Jul Withdrawal begins Rajasthan ~1 Sep NE Monsoon Tamil Nadu Oct–Dec
SUMMARYDifferential heating → ITCZ northward shift → Thar Deep Low → SW moisture-laden winds (two branches). Kerala onset ~1 Jun; full India ~15 Jul. Easterly Jet = onset burst trigger.
T-03 Yavanika — Ancient Indian Theatre
Key Questions

What is Yavanika?

Why is it named after Yavana?

Name the foundational text of Indian performing arts.

Keywords Stage curtain Yavana = Greek Natyashastra Sutradhara
Yavanika = stage curtain ONLY. Not a musical instrument, not a character, not royal patronage.

Definition

Yavanika = the stage curtain in ancient Indian theatre. Concealed actors before entrances; demarcated backstage from performance area.

Derived from Yavana (Sanskrit for Greeks, from "Ionia"). The curtain was a Hellenistic theatrical innovation introduced after Alexander's campaigns and Indo-Greek kingdoms. Indians named it after its Greek originators.

SUMMARYYavanika = stage curtain; derived from "Yavana" (Greek). Hellenistic theatrical innovation. Not an instrument or character — the answer is simply "stage curtain".

NOT all FRs automatically suspended. Art 20 & 21 can NEVER be suspended — even by Presidential order.

44th Amendment, 1978 — Key Changes

  • "Internal disturbance" → "Armed rebellion" (higher threshold)
  • Written Cabinet recommendation required before Presidential proclamation
  • Parliament approval within 1 month by special majority (was 2 months)
  • Emergency can be revoked by simple majority of Lok Sabha
  • Arts 358 restricted to war/external aggression ONLY (not armed rebellion)
  • Arts 20 & 21 can NEVER be suspended — added by 44th Amendment
  • Right to Property (Art 31) → legal right under Art 300A
SUMMARYArt 358 = automatic Art 19 suspension (war/external aggression only). Art 359 = Presidential order for other FRs. Arts 20 & 21 can NEVER be suspended. 44th Amendment was response to 1975–77 Emergency abuse.

Repechage

French: "to fish out again." A format that gives athletes who lost in early rounds a second chance — triggered when the athlete they lost to reaches the final. Those athletes compete for two bronze medals (one per semi-final side). Used in wrestling, judo, rowing, taekwondo.
