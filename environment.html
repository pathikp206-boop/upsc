Environment & Ecology

Ecosystems, biodiversity, climate change conventions, protected areas, and environmental laws

01

Ecology Fundamentals

Exam Focus: Food chains, trophic levels, ecological pyramids, energy flow, biogeochemical cycles — very frequently asked in Prelims.
ECOSYSTEM

Ecosystem Types

  • Natural: Forest, Grassland, Desert, Aquatic (Marine, Freshwater)
  • Artificial: Cropland, Plantation, Aquarium, Garden
  • Biotic (living) + Abiotic (non-living) components
  • Producers → Primary Consumers → Secondary → Tertiary
  • Energy flow: Unidirectional; ~10% transfer (10% law — Lindeman)
  • Biogeochemical cycles: Carbon, Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Water
BIODIVERSITY

Biodiversity — Types & Hotspots

  • Alpha (within habitat), Beta (between habitats), Gamma (regional)
  • India's 4 Biodiversity Hotspots: Western Ghats, Eastern Himalayas, Indo-Burma, Sundaland
  • Global: 36 hotspots (CEPF/Conservation International)
  • India — 17 megadiverse countries (5th most biodiverse globally)
  • CBD (Convention on Biological Diversity) — 1992, Rio
  • Nagoya Protocol — Access and Benefit Sharing (ABS)
BIOMES

Major Biomes of World

  • Tropical Rainforest — Amazon, Congo, SE Asia (highest biodiversity)
  • Taiga (Boreal Forest) — Conifer; Siberia, Canada
  • Tundra — permafrost; polar regions
  • Savanna — Africa; seasonal rainfall
  • Chaparral (Mediterranean) — shrubland; fire-adapted
  • Mangroves — coastal; rich biodiversity; carbon sinks
02

Protected Area Network in India

CATEGORIES

Protected Area Types

  • National Parks — strictly protected; human activities prohibited; 106 in India
  • Wildlife Sanctuaries — some human activity allowed; 567 in India
  • Biosphere Reserves — 18 in India; UNESCO: 12 listed
  • Tiger Reserves — Project Tiger (1973); 54 reserves
  • Ramsar Sites — wetlands of international importance; 80 in India
FAMOUS PARKS

Key National Parks

  • Jim Corbett — first NP of India (1936), Uttarakhand
  • Kaziranga — one-horned rhino, Assam (UNESCO WHS)
  • Sundarbans — mangrove, tiger; WB + Bangladesh
  • Gir — only wild Asiatic lions, Gujarat
  • Keoladeo Ghana — migratory birds, Rajasthan (UNESCO WHS)
  • Silent Valley — Kerala; no roads; pristine rainforest
CONSERVATION

Species Conservation Projects

  • Project Tiger (1973) — 54 tiger reserves; NTCA oversight
  • Project Elephant (1992) — 33 elephant reserves
  • Project Snow Leopard (2009) — trans-Himalayan landscapes
  • Project Crocodile (1975) — Gharial, Mugger, Saltwater Croc
  • Sea Turtle Conservation — Olive Ridley (Odisha coast)
  • Project Hangul (Kashmir Stag) — Dachigam NP

04

Indian Environmental Laws

ACTS

Key Environmental Acts

  • Wildlife Protection Act (1972) — Protected area network; schedules I–VI
  • Water Act (1974) — Prevention & control of water pollution; PCBs
  • Air Act (1981) — Control of air pollution; CPCB/SPCB
  • Environment Protection Act (1986) — Umbrella legislation; post-Bhopal
  • Forest Conservation Act (1980) — Diversion of forest land requires Centre's approval
  • Biological Diversity Act (2002) — National Biodiversity Authority
DISASTER

Disaster Management

  • Disaster Management Act (2005)
  • NDMA — National Disaster Management Authority (PM as chairman)
  • NDRF — National Disaster Response Force (16 battalions)
  • Sendai Framework (2015–2030) — 4 priorities; UNDRR
  • India's disaster risk: cyclones, floods, earthquakes, landslides
  • Seismic zones: I to V (V = most severe); Zone V — NE India, J&K, Kutch
