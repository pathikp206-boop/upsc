01

Ancient India

Exam Focus: Indus Valley Civilisation, Vedic Period, Mahajanapadas, Mauryan Empire, Gupta Age — frequently tested in Prelims. Focus on dates, capitals, and key rulers.
3000 – 1500 BCE

Indus Valley Civilisation

  • Major sites: Harappa, Mohenjodaro, Lothal (Gujarat — dockyard)
  • Kalibangan (fire altars), Dholavira (signboard)
  • Town planning: grid pattern, drainage system
  • Pashupati seal — proto-Shiva figure
  • No evidence of temples or palaces
1500 – 600 BCE

Vedic Period

  • Early Vedic: Rigveda, tribal society, pastoral economy
  • Later Vedic: agriculture, Painted Grey Ware (PGW)
  • 16 Mahajanapadas — Magadha, Kuru, Panchala key ones
  • Upanishads — philosophical treatises (Vedanta)
  • Buddhism & Jainism founded ~6th century BCE
321 – 185 BCE

Mauryan Empire

  • Founded by Chandragupta Maurya (321 BCE)
  • Kautilya's Arthashastra — treatise on governance
  • Ashoka (268–232 BCE) — Kalinga War, Dhamma
  • Rock Edicts, Pillar Edicts — spread of Buddhism
  • Capital: Pataliputra. Megasthenes — Greek ambassador
320 – 550 CE

Gupta Empire — Golden Age

  • Chandragupta I → Samudragupta → Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya)
  • Allahabad Pillar Inscription — Samudragupta's conquests
  • Kalidasa (Meghaduta, Shakuntala), Aryabhata, Varahamihira
  • Nalanda University, Ajanta caves (painting flourish)
  • Iron Pillar of Delhi — rust-resistant metallurgy
600 – 647 CE

Harshavardhana

  • Capital: Kanauj. Last great empire of northern India
  • Hiuen Tsang's visit — Si-Yu-Ki text
  • Banskhera & Madhuban copper plate inscriptions
  • Harshacharita by Banabhatta
3rd–9th CE

South Indian Kingdoms

  • Cholas, Cheras, Pandyas — Sangam Age (Tamil literature)
  • Pallavas (Mahabalipuram temples, Kanchi capital)
  • Chalukyas of Vatapi (Aihole, Badami, Pattadakal)
  • Rashtrakutas — Ellora caves (Kailash Temple)
02

Medieval India

Exam Focus: Delhi Sultanate dynasties (5 in order), Mughal emperors sequence, Bhakti/Sufi movements, and administrative/revenue systems.
1206 – 1526 CE

Delhi Sultanate

  • Slave Dynasty — Qutb-ud-din Aibak, Iltutmish, Razia Sultana, Balban
  • Khilji Dynasty — Alauddin Khilji (market reforms, Mongol repulsion)
  • Tughlaq Dynasty — Muhammad bin Tughlaq (token currency, capital shift to Daulatabad)
  • Sayyid & Lodi — Ibrahim Lodi defeated at Panipat I (1526)
  • Iqta system, Diwan-i-Riyasat, Diwan-i-Bandagan
1526 – 1857 CE

Mughal Empire

  • Babur (1526) → Humayun → Akbar → Jahangir → Shah Jahan → Aurangzeb
  • Akbar — Din-i-Ilahi, Mansabdari system, Rajput policy, Abul Fazl (Ain-i-Akbari)
  • Shah Jahan — Taj Mahal, Red Fort, Peacock Throne
  • Aurangzeb — Jizya re-imposed; Maratha rise
  • Todar Mal — land revenue (Zabti system)
1674 – 1818 CE

Maratha Empire

  • Shivaji — Raigad capital, Ashta Pradhan (8 ministers)
  • Chauth & Sardeshmukhi — revenue levies
  • Peshwas — Bajirao I (greatest expansion)
  • Battle of Panipat III (1761) — Afghan victory over Marathas
  • Anglo-Maratha Wars (3) → British supremacy by 1818
8th–17th CE

Bhakti & Sufi Movements

  • Alvars & Nayanmars — Tamil Bhakti (south, early)
  • Kabir, Ramananda, Mirabai, Tukaram, Chaitanya
  • Sufi Orders: Chishti (Muinuddin Chishti, Nizamuddin Auliya), Suhrawardi, Naqshbandi, Qadiri
  • Guru Nanak — Sikhism (1469); Ten Gurus lineage
03

Modern India & Freedom Struggle

Exam Focus: British expansion timeline, Governor Generals' acts, major revolts, Congress Sessions, Gandhi's movements — VERY high weightage in both Prelims & Mains.
1600
East India Company founded
Charter granted by Queen Elizabeth I. Trading posts at Surat (1608), Madras (1639), Bombay (1661), Calcutta (1690).
1757
Battle of Plassey
Clive defeats Siraj-ud-Daula. Mir Jafar placed as Nawab. Beginning of British political dominance.
1857
First War of Independence / Sepoy Mutiny
Mangal Pandey sparks revolt. Centres: Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur, Lucknow, Jhansi. Rani Lakshmibai, Nana Saheb. Marks end of East India Company rule — Crown takes over.
1885
Indian National Congress founded
A.O. Hume. First session Bombay, W.C. Bonnerjee president. Three phases: Moderate (1885–1905), Extremist, Gandhian.
1905
Partition of Bengal & Swadeshi Movement
Viceroy Curzon divides Bengal. Bal Pal Lal (Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Bipin Pal, Lala Lajpat Rai) lead Swadeshi agitation.
1919
Jallianwala Bagh Massacre
April 13 — General Dyer orders firing at Amritsar gathering. 379+ killed. Rabindranath Tagore returns knighthood.
1920–22
Non-Cooperation Movement
Gandhi's first mass movement. Boycott of British goods/titles/courts/schools. Withdrawn after Chauri Chaura violence (1922).
1930
Civil Disobedience / Salt March (Dandi March)
March 12 – April 6, 241 miles from Sabarmati Ashram to Dandi. Gandhi breaks salt law. Gandhi-Irwin Pact (1931) follows.
1942
Quit India Movement
"Do or Die" — August 8, Bombay. Mass arrests. Underground parallel govts in Satara, Midnapore, Ballia. Most intense movement.
1947
Independence & Partition
August 15 — Indian Independence. Mountbatten Plan (June 3). India & Pakistan created. Radcliffe Line drawn.

Key Acts & Policies

Act / Policy Year Significance
Regulating Act1773First step in parliamentary control over EIC; Governor-General post created (Warren Hastings first)
Pitts India Act1784Board of Control established; dual governance
Charter Act1833EIC lost trading rights; India became a colony; Law Commission (Macaulay)
Doctrine of Lapse1848Dalhousie — states without natural heir annexed (Satara, Nagpur, Jhansi)
Indian Councils Act1861Legislative councils; Indians nominated (Syed Ahmad Khan influence)
Morley-Minto Reforms1909Separate electorate for Muslims — seeds of partition
Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms1919Dyarchy in provinces; GIA Act 1919
Government of India Act1935Provincial autonomy; Federal structure; basis of Indian Constitution
Indian Independence Act1947British Parliament act ending Crown rule; creating two dominions
