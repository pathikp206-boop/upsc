Indian Art & Culture

Architecture, sculpture, painting, music, dance, literature, and performing arts — rich tradition from Vedic to modern era

01

Indian Architecture

Exam Focus: Temple architecture styles (Nagara vs Dravidian vs Vesara), cave architecture, UNESCO World Heritage Sites — very high Prelims weightage.
TEMPLE STYLES

Temple Architecture Styles

  • Nagara (North) — Curvilinear shikhara (tower); Brihadeeswara, Khajuraho, Konark
  • Dravidian (South) — Pyramidal vimana; gopuram gateway; Brihadeeswarar, Shore Temple
  • Vesara (Mixed) — Chalukya & Hoysala blend; Belur, Halebid, Badami
  • Parts: Garbhagriha (sanctum), Mandapa (hall), Shikhara/Vimana (tower)
CAVE TEMPLES

Rock-Cut Caves

  • Ajanta (Maharashtra) — Buddhist; paintings (2nd BCE–6th CE); UNESCO WHS
  • Ellora (Maharashtra) — Hindu, Buddhist, Jain; Kailash Temple (Rashtrakuta)
  • Elephanta (Mumbai) — Shiva caves; 6th–8th CE; UNESCO WHS
  • Mahabalipuram — Pallava; Pancha Rathas (monolithic)
  • Badami — Chalukya; 4 cave temples (3 Hindu, 1 Jain)
MUGHAL ARCH

Mughal Architecture

  • Babur — introduced Char-bagh (four-garden) layout
  • Humayun's Tomb (1570) — precursor to Taj Mahal (Delhi; UNESCO)
  • Akbar — Fatehpur Sikri, Agra Fort, Buland Darwaza
  • Shah Jahan — Taj Mahal, Red Fort Delhi, Jama Masjid, Moti Masjid
  • Taj Mahal — white marble, pietra dura (inlay work); UNESCO WHS
UNESCO — INDIA

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

  • Cultural: 35 sites — Taj Mahal, Ajanta, Ellora, Hampi, Qutb Minar, Sanchi, Red Fort, etc.
  • Natural: 7 sites — Kaziranga, Keoladeo, Sundarbans, Nanda Devi, Western Ghats, Great Himalayan NP, Manas
  • Mixed: 1 — Khangchendzonga NP (Sikkim)
  • Total: 43 UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India (2024)

03

Indian Painting Schools

MUGHAL

Mughal Paintings

  • Founded by Humayun — brought Persian painters (Mir Sayyid Ali, Abd al-Samad)
  • Akbar — Hamzanama (1400 paintings); Persian + Indian fusion
  • Jahangir — peak; naturalistic; portraits; Ustad Mansur (birds & animals)
  • Features: fine brushwork, flat perspective, nature/hunt/court themes
RAJASTHANI

Rajput / Rajasthani Schools

  • Mewar, Marwar, Bundi, Kishangarh, Jaipur schools
  • Bold colours, large eyes, Krishna-Radha themes
  • Kishangarh — Bani Thani painting ("Mona Lisa of India")
  • Mewar — Chawand and Nathdwara sub-styles
FOLK ART

Folk & Tribal Painting

  • Madhubani (Bihar) — geometric patterns, mythological themes; practiced by women
  • Warli (Maharashtra) — tribal; white on red/brown; daily life
  • Pattachitra (Odisha/WB) — scroll paintings; Jagannath themes
  • Gond (MP/Chhattisgarh) — nature, animals; dots/dashes
  • Kalamkari (AP) — pen/block on cotton; epics
  • Phad (Rajasthan) — narrative scroll; Pabuji/Devnarayan epics
04

Classical Music & Literature

MUSIC

Indian Classical Music

  • Hindustani (North) — Persian influence; Khayal, Dhrupad, Thumri
  • Carnatic (South) — Bhakti tradition; Thyagaraja, Muthuswami Dikshitar, Syama Sastri (Trinity)
  • Raga — melodic framework; Tala — rhythmic cycle
  • Samavedic origin; Natyashastra by Bharata (music theory)
LITERATURE

Indian Literature Highlights

  • Sangam literature — Tamil; 8 anthologies (Ettuthokai), 10 idylls (Pattupattu)
  • Sanskrit: Ramayana (Valmiki), Mahabharata (Vyasa), Kalidasa's plays
  • Pali/Prakrit — Buddhist/Jain texts; Jataka Tales
  • Persian: Ain-i-Akbari (Abul Fazl), Baburnama
  • Nobel in Literature: Rabindranath Tagore (1913) — Gitanjali
PUPPETRY

Puppetry & Theatre

  • String: Katputli (Rajasthan), Gombeyatta (Karnataka)
  • Shadow: Tholu Bommalata (AP), Togalu Gombeyaata (Karnataka)
  • Rod: Putul Nautch (WB), Kundhei (Odisha)
  • Glove: Pavakoothu (Kerala)
  • Yakshagana (Karnataka) — folk theatre; elaborate costumes
  • Therukoothu (Tamil Nadu) — street theatre
