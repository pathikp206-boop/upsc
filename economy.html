Indian Economy

Planning, monetary policy, banking, sectors, schemes, and international trade — essential for GS III and Essay

01

National Income & Economic Concepts

Exam Focus: GDP vs GNP vs NNP, types of inflation, fiscal deficit definitions — basic but tricky in Prelims options.
NATIONAL INCOME

GDP, GNP & NNP

  • GDP = Total market value of goods & services within borders
  • GNP = GDP + Net factor income from abroad
  • NNP = GNP − Depreciation (Net National Product)
  • NNP at factor cost = National Income
  • Base year for India's GDP: 2011-12 (current series)
  • Measured at Market Price or Factor Cost
INFLATION

Types of Inflation

  • CPI (Consumer Price Index) — MPC target: 4% (±2%)
  • WPI (Wholesale Price Index) — for producer level
  • Repo Rate — rate at which RBI lends to banks
  • Reverse Repo — rate at which RBI borrows from banks
  • SLR — Statutory Liquidity Ratio (banks invest in govt securities)
  • CRR — Cash Reserve Ratio (kept with RBI, earns no interest)
FISCAL CONCEPTS

Budget Deficits

  • Revenue Deficit = Revenue Expenditure − Revenue Receipts
  • Fiscal Deficit = Total Expenditure − Total Receipts (excl. borrowings)
  • Primary Deficit = Fiscal Deficit − Interest Payments
  • CAD (Current Account Deficit) — imports > exports
  • FRBM Act (2003) — fiscal consolidation target
PLANNING

Planning in India

  • Planning Commission (1950) → replaced by NITI Aayog (2015)
  • 12 Five Year Plans (1951–2017); 12th Plan ended 2017
  • 1st Plan — Harrod-Domar model; priority to agriculture
  • 2nd Plan — Mahalanobis model; heavy industries
  • NITI Aayog — think tank; Governing Council includes CMs
02

Banking & Financial System

RBI

Reserve Bank of India

  • Established 1935 (Hilton Young Commission); nationalised 1949
  • Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) — 6 members; inflation target
  • Banker's bank, Government's bank, lender of last resort
  • Issues currency (except Re. 1 coin — Ministry of Finance)
  • Regulates NBFCs, Co-operative banks, Payment banks
BANKING TYPES

Types of Banks

  • Commercial Banks — SBI (largest PSB), HDFC, ICICI
  • Regional Rural Banks (RRBs) — rural credit since 1975
  • Cooperative Banks — multi-tier (State, District, Village)
  • Payment Banks — limited services, Paytm, Airtel, India Post
  • Small Finance Banks — underserved segments
  • NABARD — apex body for rural credit
CAPITAL MARKET

Capital & Securities Market

  • SEBI (1988) — regulates securities market; statutory body 1992
  • BSE (1875) — oldest stock exchange in Asia
  • NSE — largest by turnover; established 1992
  • Primary market (IPO) vs Secondary market (trading)
  • FDI vs FPI (FII) — direct investment vs portfolio
03

Sectors & Economic Survey

SECTORS

Three Sectors of Economy

  • Primary — Agriculture, Forestry, Mining (~16% of GDP)
  • Secondary — Manufacturing, Industry (~28% of GDP)
  • Tertiary/Services — IT, banking, trade (~55% of GDP)
  • India — service-led growth (unlike typical development path)
  • Agriculture employs ~45% of workforce
TRADE

International Trade

  • WTO — dispute resolution, most favoured nation principle
  • India's largest export: Engineering goods, Petroleum products
  • India's largest import: Petroleum, Gold, Electronics
  • Largest trading partner: USA (exports); China (imports)
  • Special Economic Zones (SEZs) — export-led growth
SCHEMES

Key Economic Schemes

  • PM-KISAN — ₹6000/year direct income to farmers
  • MGNREGA — 100 days guaranteed rural employment
  • Startup India / Stand Up India — entrepreneurship
  • Make in India — manufacturing hub (2014)
  • PLI Scheme — Production Linked Incentive (14 sectors)
  • Jan Dhan Yojana — financial inclusion (2014)
