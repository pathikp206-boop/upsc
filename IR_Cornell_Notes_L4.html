Full form: Biodiversity Beyond National Jurisdiction Agreement Developed under: UNCLOS Coverage: Beyond 200 nautical miles (beyond EEZ) India: Signed (25 Sep 2024) but NOT ratified As of Nov 2024: 105 signed, only 15 ratified

UNCLOS Implementing Agreements (3 total)

1. 1994 Part XI Implementation Agreement — seabed minerals 2. 1995 UN Fish Stocks Agreement — migratory fish conservation 3. BBNJ Agreement (2023) — high seas biodiversity [NEW]

Key Facts

  • High seas cover 64% of ocean surface and 43% of Earth's area
  • Host 2.2 million marine species and vast microbial life
  • 17 million tonnes of plastic dumped in oceans in 2021
  • Enters international law 120 days after 60 countries ratify
  • Supports SDG 14 (Life Below Water)

4 Objectives of BBNJ

ObjectiveDetails
Marine Protected Areas (MPAs)Establish conservation zones to protect ecosystems from human activities and climate change
Marine Genetic ResourcesEquitable sharing of benefits from marine genetic discoveries (e.g., drug development) with open access to research
Environmental Impact Assessments (EIA)Prior EIAs mandated for activities that could harm marine ecosystems, with public disclosure
Capacity Building & Technology TransferSupports small island and landlocked nations in conservation efforts and sustainable resource use
SummaryBBNJ = Biodiversity Beyond National Jurisdiction. 3rd UNCLOS implementing agreement (after 1994 Seabed minerals + 1995 Fish Stocks). Covers beyond 200nm EEZ. High seas = 64% ocean surface, 43% Earth's area. Enters into force 120 days after 60 ratifications (only 15 ratified as of Nov 2024). India signed Sep 2024, not ratified.

Adopted: 10 Dec 1982, Montego Bay, Jamaica In force: 16 Nov 1994 (after 60 ratifications) 168 parties. India = State Party. AKA "Constitution for the Oceans"

5 Maritime Zones

ZoneDistanceRightsAirspace?
Internal WatersLandward of baselineFull sovereignty (bays, ports, rivers). NO right of innocent passage.Yes (full sovereignty)
Territorial Sea12 nmFull sovereignty over surface, seabed, subsoil. Allows innocent passage.Yes
Contiguous Zone24 nmLimited control — fiscal, immigration, sanitary, customs laws only.NO airspace jurisdiction
EEZ200 nmSovereign rights for resource exploration, conservation, energy production. Does NOT restrict navigation or overflight.No (international airspace)
High SeasBeyond EEZOpen to all states for peaceful purposes. "Common heritage of all mankind."International airspace
Continental Shelf: Beyond 200nm EEZ up to 350nm — coastal state has sovereign rights over seabed/subsoil resources (managed by CLCS).

3 Institutions Created by UNCLOS

1. ITLOS (International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea) — disputes on UNCLOS interpretation 2. ISA (International Seabed Authority) — protects marine environment from deep-seabed activities 3. CLCS (Commission on the Limits of the Continental Shelf) — sets outer limits of continental shelf beyond 200nm

South China Sea Disputes

FeatureClaimants
Spratly IslandsChina, Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia, Brunei, Taiwan
Paracel IslandsChina, Vietnam, Taiwan
Scarborough ShoalChina, Philippines, Taiwan
Nine-Dash LineChina's claim encompassing ~90% of South China Sea (based on historical maps). NOT recognized by international law. Permanent Court of Arbitration (2016): ruled against China — China rejected the ruling.
Grey Zone TacticsChina uses Coast Guard + maritime militia for aggressive maneuvers, vessel harassment — altering status quo without direct warfare.
SummaryUNCLOS (adopted 10 Dec 1982, in force 16 Nov 1994). 5 zones: Internal Waters → TS (12nm) → CZ (24nm, no airspace) → EEZ (200nm) → High Seas. 3 institutions: ITLOS, ISA, CLCS. South China Sea: Spratlys (6 claimants), Paracel (3), Scarborough Shoal (3). Nine-Dash Line = ~90% SCS claim, rejected by international law.
