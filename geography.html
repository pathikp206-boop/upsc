01

Physical Geography of India

Exam Focus: Physiographic divisions, Himalayan rivers vs Peninsular rivers, monsoon mechanism, soil types, natural vegetation — very frequently tested.
PHYSIOGRAPHY

5 Physiographic Divisions

  • Himalayan Mountains — Trans-Himalaya, Greater, Lesser, Shiwaliks
  • Northern Plains — Bhabar, Terai, Khadar (new), Bhangar (old)
  • Peninsular Plateau — Deccan, Central Highlands, NE plateaus
  • Coastal Plains — Eastern (wider, deltas) vs Western (narrow, lagoons)
  • Islands — Andaman & Nicobar (Bay of Bengal), Lakshadweep (Arabian Sea)
HIMALAYAN RIVERS

Himalayan River System

  • Indus — Tibet origin; Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Sutlej (tributaries)
  • Ganga — Gangotri; tributaries: Yamuna, Ghaghra, Gandak, Kosi
  • Brahmaputra — Mansarovar; Tsangpo in Tibet; Dihang gorge in Arunachal
  • Antecedent drainage — older than Himalayas
  • Carry more silt → fertile plains
PENINSULAR RIVERS

Peninsular River System

  • West-flowing: Narmada, Tapi (rift valleys, estuaries)
  • East-flowing: Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, Cauvery (deltas)
  • Godavari — "Vridha Ganga", longest peninsular river
  • Cauvery — "Dakshina Ganga"
  • Non-perennial, rain-fed; no glacial source
MONSOON

Indian Monsoon Mechanism

  • SW Monsoon (June–Sept) — Arabian Sea branch + Bay of Bengal branch
  • ITCZ shifts northward → pressure differential
  • El Niño — weakens monsoon; La Niña — strengthens
  • Western Disturbances → winter rain NW India
  • NE Monsoon (Oct–Dec) → Tamil Nadu gets rain
SOILS

Soil Types in India

  • Alluvial — most fertile, 43% area (Ganga-Indus plains, coastal)
  • Black/Regur — cotton soil, Deccan trap, moisture retentive
  • Red & Yellow — poor nitrogen, Eastern Deccan
  • Laterite — leaching, Karnataka, Kerala, hill areas
  • Arid/Desert — Rajasthan; Saline soils — Rann of Kutch
VEGETATION

Natural Vegetation Zones

  • Tropical Rainforest — Andaman, Western Ghats (>200cm rainfall)
  • Tropical Deciduous — most common; Teak, Sal, Bamboo
  • Tropical Thorn — Rajasthan, less than 70cm; Acacia, Cacti
  • Montane — Coniferous above 1800m; Alpine above 3600m
  • Mangroves — Sundarbans, Mahanadi delta
02

Climate, Minerals & Resources

MINERALS

Key Mineral Reserves

  • Iron Ore — Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh (Bailadila)
  • Coal — Jharkhand (Jharia), W. Bengal (Raniganj)
  • Bauxite — Odisha (Panchpatmali), Jharkhand
  • Copper — Rajasthan (Khetri), Jharkhand (Singhbhum)
  • Mica — Jharkhand, Andhra Pradesh (largest producer)
  • Petroleum — Mumbai High, Assam (Digboi — first refinery 1901)
  • Manganese — Odisha (largest reserves)
CLIMATE ZONES

Climatic Types (Köppen Modified)

  • Tropical Monsoon — Most of India (Am)
  • Tropical Savanna — Deccan Plateau (Aw)
  • Semi-Arid Steppe — Rajasthan fringe (BSh)
  • Hot Desert — Thar Desert (BWh)
  • Humid Subtropical — North India (Cwa)
  • Highland — Himalayas (varies with altitude)
  • Cherrapunji — highest rainfall area (Meghalaya)

04

Agriculture in India

KHARIF CROPS

Kharif (Jun–Sep)

  • Rice — WB, UP, AP, Punjab
  • Cotton — Gujarat, Maharashtra, AP
  • Jowar, Bajra, Maize — Maharashtra, Karnataka
  • Groundnut — Gujarat (largest), AP
  • Soybean — MP
  • Jute — WB (largest), Bihar
RABI CROPS

Rabi (Oct–Mar)

  • Wheat — UP (largest), Punjab, Haryana
  • Mustard — Rajasthan, UP, Haryana
  • Gram/Chickpea — MP, Rajasthan
  • Linseed, Barley — central India
  • Requires western disturbances for rain
CASH CROPS

Plantation & Cash Crops

  • Tea — Assam, WB (Darjeeling), Tamil Nadu
  • Coffee — Karnataka (Coorg), Kerala, Tamil Nadu
  • Rubber — Kerala (90% production)
  • Sugarcane — UP (largest), Maharashtra
  • Spices — Kerala (cardamom, pepper)
