India & Immediate Neighbourhood
📌 Summary — India–Pakistan (2025–26)

Bilateral relations at their lowest point following the April 2025 Pahalgam terror attack and India's retaliatory Operation Sindoor (May 2025). Formal trade is completely frozen (200% duty on indirect goods). Diplomatic engagement limited to multilateral forums (SCO). Kartarpur Corridor remains the sole open channel.

01
Operation Sindoor
May 2025 · Cross-border precision strikes
Security
Key Facts
  • Triggered by deadly terror attack in April 2025
  • India precision-struck LeT HQ (Muridke) & JeM HQ (Bahawalpur) — Rafales with SCALP-EG ALCMs, no LoC crossing
  • Pakistan retaliated with swarm drones/UCAVs — foiled by Akash & QRSAM
  • S-400 locked airspace during 114-aircraft standoff; PAF jets shot down
⚠ Nomenclature Trap

Operation Sindoor (2025, LoC strike) ≠ Operation Bandar (2019, Balakot airstrikes) ≠ Operation Sunrise (India–Myanmar joint op)

02
Indus Waters Treaty
Signed 19 Sept 1960, Karachi
Water Diplomacy
River Division
  • India (Eastern): Sutlej, Beas, Ravi → ~20% water
  • Pakistan (Western): Indus, Jhelum, Chenab → ~80% water
  • India allowed domestic use, limited agriculture & run-of-river hydropower from Western rivers
Key Projects (J&K)
  • Kishenganga — 330 MW, Bandipora, Jhelum tributary; Pakistan contests drawdown flushing
  • Ratle — 850 MW, Kishtwar, Chenab; under construction; Pakistan objects to freeboard height
Dispute Resolution (Graded)
  • PIC → Neutral Expert (NE) → Court of Arbitration (CoA)
  • 2025 ruling: NE Michel Lino confirmed jurisdiction — validates India's graded approach
  • India served modification notices under Art. XII(3) (Jan 2023, Aug 2024) citing climate change, terrorism, demographic shifts
⚠ Key Traps

No unilateral exit (Art. XII). World Bank = signatory for dispute settlement only, not a guarantor of water flow. "Abeyance" ≠ "Modification."

India–Bangladesh
📌 Summary — Bangladesh (2024–26)

Political earthquake (July–Aug 2024): student protests → PM Hasina fled to India → Interim Govt under Dr. Muhammad Yunus. BNP won Feb 2026 elections; Awami League banned. Bangladesh seeks Hasina's extradition. Bilateral ties hostile — visa freeze, Indian missions attacked.

India–Myanmar
05
FMR Scrapping & Border Fencing
1,643 km border
Security
FMR Facts
  • FMR implemented 1968; allowed 16 km cross-border movement without visa
  • Scrapped by MHA; entire border to be fenced; biometric verification + border passes (7 days) replacing FMR
  • Reasons: demographic shifts, insurgent safe havens, narcotics (Golden Triangle), weapons
Key Projects & Issues
  • Kaladan Multi-Modal: Kolkata → Sittwe (sea) → Paletwa (Kaladan river) → Mizoram. Severely delayed — Arakan Army captured Paletwa 2024–25
  • IMT Trilateral Highway: Moreh (Manipur) → Mae Sot (Thailand); stalled — Sagaing Region conflict zone
  • Over 2,100 Indians rescued from cyber slavery camps since 2022
  • Operation Brahma: India's humanitarian aid during March 2025 Myanmar earthquake
⚠ Operation Trap

Operation Sunrise = 2019 joint India–Myanmar Army op to bust insurgent camps (now impossible — Tatmadaw lost border control).

India–USA
COMPACT + Interim Trade Agreement
Major Powers
COMPACT (Feb 2025 Modi visit)
  • "Catalyzing Opportunities for Military Partnership, Accelerated Commerce & Technology"
  • 10-year Defence Framework (2025–2035); Javelin ATGMs + Stryker ICV co-production
  • Mission 500: double bilateral trade to $500B by 2030 (currently $132B FY25)
  • US-India TRUST (critical tech); iCET: GE Aerospace + HAL → F414 engines in India (NSCs nodal, NOT ministries)
  • NISAR (NASA–ISRO SAR satellite); India signed Artemis Accords (shifted from Russia space diplomacy)
  • Tahawwur Rana extradition approved (26/11 link)
2026 Interim Trade Agreement (NOT FTA)
  • US tariffs on Indian goods slashed from 50% → 18% (0% for gems, pharma, silk)
  • Russian oil penalty (extra 25%) lifted
  • India reduced tariffs on US industrial goods + select agri (tree nuts, apples, wine)
  • India's red lines: Dairy, Poultry, Cereals (Rice/Wheat), Cotton — fully protected
  • DDGS import allowed but US corn/maize & fuel ethanol banned
  • India to "intend" (not commit) to procure $500B of US goods over 5 years
  • GSP revoked 2019 — NOT yet restored
  • FORGE: US-led critical minerals initiative targeting China's rare earth monopoly (~70% mining, ~90% processing)
Other Key Points
  • SOSA Pact: Non-binding industrial supply chain mechanism; India = US's 18th SOSA partner
  • H-1B: Random lottery scrapped, massive fee hikes; restricting Indian STEM talent
  • China's counter: K-Visa (no employer sponsorship needed, unlike H-1B)

India & Europe
18
EU & Eurozone Dynamics
Key Developments 2025–26
Europe
Structural Facts
  • EU = 27 members; established 1 Nov 1993 (Maastricht Treaty)
  • Eurozone: 21 members (Bulgaria joined 1 Jan 2026 — replaced Lev; 21st Eurozone member)
  • Schengen: Romania & Bulgaria became full members 1 Jan 2025
  • Schengen ≠ EU: Non-EU Schengen = Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein; EU not in Schengen = Ireland & Cyprus
CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism)
  • Definitive phase: 1 Jan 2026 — EU importers pay carbon tax on carbon-intensive goods
  • 6 targeted sectors: Iron & Steel, Aluminium, Cement, Fertilizers, Electricity, Hydrogen
  • India's steel & aluminium exports heavily impacted
  • India protested at WTO as non-tariff barrier; invokes CBDR principle (Common But Differentiated Responsibilities)
India–EU Key Mechanisms
  • TTC (Trade & Technology Council): EU holds TTC with only 2 countries — US and India
  • BTIA negotiations active; blocked by EU refusing India "Data Secure Nation" status under GDPR
