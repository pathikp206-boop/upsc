CPTPP

12 members (original 11 + UK) Chair 2026: Vietnam (from Australia) USA NOT a member (withdrew from TPP 2017) India NOT a member UK = 12th member (Dec 15, 2024) — first European, first non-founding member
Americas: Canada, Mexico, Peru, Chile Asia-Pacific: Japan, Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore, Brunei, Australia, New Zealand Europe: UK
Pact now spans the Atlantic (via UK) — no longer just "Trans-Pacific." China & Taiwan both applied 2021 — stalled. Auckland Principles require CONSENSUS, so any member (Japan/Canada) can block China's entry. India prefers bilateral deals (UK, EU, Peru). India opposes ISDS mechanism in CPTPP.

Auckland Principles (3 Criteria)

1. High standards (must match existing rules) 2. Track record of trade compliance 3. Consensus of all existing members

RCEP

ParameterDetails
FormationTalks: Nov 2012 (Cambodia). Signed: Nov 15, 2020 (Vietnam). In force: Jan 1, 2022. Philippines = last founding member to ratify (June 2023).
Members (15)ASEAN (10): Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam. Partners (5): China, Japan, South Korea, Australia, New Zealand.
Scale~30% Global GDP + ~30% Global Population. Largest FTA in history.
SecretariatJakarta, Indonesia (within ASEAN Secretariat complex)
India's ExitNov 4, 2019 (Bangkok Summit). Reason 1 (Auto-Trigger): India demanded automatic tariff hike if Chinese imports surged — RCEP rejected. Reason 2 (Ratchet Mechanism): India opposed mechanism preventing nations from raising tariffs once lowered.

SummaryCPTPP: 12 members (UK = 12th, Dec 2024). Chair 2026: Vietnam. US & India NOT members. Auckland Principles = consensus → any member blocks China. RCEP: 15 members (largest FTA), in force Jan 2022. India exited Nov 4, 2019 (Auto-Trigger + Ratchet). RCEP Secretariat: Jakarta.

PTAFTACECACEPA Customs UnionCommon MarketEconomic Union (Deepest)
TermFull FormOne-LinerTrap / Example
FTAFree Trade AgreementReduce/eliminate tariffs on "Negative List" basisUsually covers Goods; Services less comprehensive than CEPA. Ex: India-ASEAN FTA.
CECAComprehensive Economic Cooperation Agreement"Tariffs + Cooperation": FTA + customs/standards cooperationDeeper than FTA, less than CEPA. Ex: India-Singapore CECA.
CEPAComprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement"The Gold Standard": Goods + Services + Investment + IPR + Competition Law + Govt ProcurementTreats partner almost like domestic state. Ex: India-UAE CEPA, India-Japan CEPA.
TEPATrade and Economic Partnership Agreement"Investment-Led": CEPA-like + unique binding $100Bn investment commitmentOnly Indian deal with legally binding "Investment Target" from partner. Ex: India-EFTA TEPA.
BITBilateral Investment Treaty"Protection Only": Protects FDI from expropriation. Does NOT lower tariffs on goods.Israel = first OECD country to adopt India's new BIT model. Recent BITs: Uzbekistan (2024), UAE (2024), Kyrgyzstan (2025).
SPSSanitary and Phytosanitary Measures"Health Barriers": Rules to protect human/animal/plant healthUsed by EU/USA as Non-Tariff Barrier to reject Indian agri-exports (e.g., Alphonso mango ban).
NTBNon-Tariff Barrier"Invisible Wall": Any trade barrier that is not a taxAs tariffs fall (WTO), NTBs rise as new protection method. Includes quotas, SPS norms, labeling.

India's 8 FTAs Since 2021

1. Mauritius CECPA (Apr 2021) — 1st Africa FTA 2. UAE CEPA (May 2022) — 88 days record 3. Australia ECTA (Dec 2022) 4. EFTA TEPA (Oct 2025) — $100Bn FDI 5. Oman CEPA (Dec 2025) — GCC gateway 6. UK CETA (2025, signed) 7. New Zealand FTA (Dec 2025, concluded) 8. EU FTA (Jan 2026, concluded)
