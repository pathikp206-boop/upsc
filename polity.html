Indian Polity & Constitution

Constitutional framework, fundamental rights, governance, panchayati raj, and amendments — the backbone of GS Paper II

01

Constitution — Key Features

Exam Focus: Sources of constitution, borrowed features, Preamble keywords, Schedules content — Prelims favourite.
PREAMBLE

Preamble Keywords

  • India is a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic
  • Socialist & Secular added by 42nd Amendment (1976)
  • Justice — Social, Economic, Political
  • Liberty — Thought, Expression, Belief, Faith, Worship
  • Equality of status and opportunity
  • Fraternity — assuring dignity, unity & integrity
  • Adopted: 26 November 1949; Effective: 26 January 1950
BORROWED FEATURES

Sources of the Constitution

  • UK — Parliamentary system, Rule of Law, Writs, Cabinet
  • USA — Fundamental Rights, Judicial Review, Preamble, FR
  • Ireland — DPSP, Nomination to RS, Method of election of President
  • Canada — Federal + strong centre, residuary powers
  • Australia — Concurrent list, joint sitting of Parliament
  • Germany — Emergency provisions
  • France — Liberty, Equality, Fraternity; Republic
  • South Africa — Amendment procedure (by 2/3rd majority)
SCHEDULES

12 Schedules

  • 1st — States & UTs list
  • 2nd — Salaries of Constitutional functionaries
  • 3rd — Oaths & Affirmations
  • 4th — Allocation of RS seats to states
  • 5th — Administration of Scheduled Areas/Tribes
  • 6th — Tribal areas (NE India)
  • 7th — Three Lists (Union, State, Concurrent)
  • 8th — 22 Official Languages
  • 9th — Acts/Regulations protected from judicial review
  • 10th — Anti-Defection Law (1985)
  • 11th — Panchayati Raj (29 subjects)
  • 12th — Municipalities (18 subjects)
BASIC STRUCTURE

Basic Structure Doctrine

  • Kesavananda Bharati case (1973) — 13-judge bench
  • Parliament cannot amend basic structure
  • Includes: Supremacy of Constitution, democratic republic, secular character, separation of powers, judicial review, federalism, parliamentary system, free and fair elections, rule of law
02

Fundamental Rights (Part III, Art 12–35)

Art 12–13
Definition of State; Laws inconsistent with FRs void
Art 14–18
Right to Equality — equality before law, no discrimination, abolition of untouchability & titles
Art 19–22
Right to Freedom — 6 freedoms (Art 19), protection against conviction (Art 20), life & liberty (Art 21), right against arrest (Art 22)
Art 21A
Right to Education (6–14 yrs) — added by 86th Amendment 2002
Art 23–24
Right Against Exploitation — prohibition of human trafficking, bonded labour; no child labour below 14
Art 25–28
Right to Freedom of Religion — practice, propagate; manage religious affairs; no religious instruction in state schools
Art 29–30
Cultural & Educational Rights — minorities may establish & administer educational institutions
Art 32–35
Right to Constitutional Remedies — 5 writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Certiorari, Prohibition, Quo Warranto
Note: Right to Property (Art 31) removed from FRs by 44th Amendment (1978) — now a legal right under Art 300A. Right to Privacy declared fundamental right in K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India (2017).
03

DPSP & Fundamental Duties

PART IV · ART 36–51

Directive Principles (DPSP)

  • Non-justiciable but fundamental to governance
  • Borrowed from Ireland (Irish Constitution)
  • Socialistic: Art 38, 39, 39A, 41, 42, 43, 43A, 47
  • Gandhian: Art 40, 43, 43B, 46, 47, 48
  • Liberal-intellectual: Art 44, 45, 48A, 49, 50, 51
  • Art 44 — Uniform Civil Code
  • Art 45 — Free & compulsory education (now FR via 21A)
  • Art 48A — Protection of environment (added by 42nd)
PART IV-A · ART 51A

Fundamental Duties (11 total)

  • Added by 42nd Amendment 1976 (originally 10)
  • 11th duty added by 86th Amendment 2002 — duty of parent to provide education to child aged 6-14
  • Based on Swaran Singh Committee recommendations
  • Non-justiciable but legally enforceable in some cases
  • Includes: uphold Constitution, defend sovereignty, protect environment, develop scientific temper

05

Parliament, President & Judiciary

PARLIAMENT

Structure of Parliament

  • Rajya Sabha — max 250 (238 elected + 12 nominated); permanent house; 1/3 retire every 2 yrs
  • Lok Sabha — max 552 (530+20+2 Anglo-Indian); 5-year term
  • Money Bill — only Lok Sabha; RS has 14 days to return
  • Joint Sitting — Art 108; presided by Lok Sabha Speaker
  • Budget presented on 1 February (changed from last day of Feb)
PRESIDENT

President of India

  • Art 52–78; elected by Electoral College (MPs + MLAs)
  • 5-year term; can be impeached by Parliament
  • Pocket Veto — no time limit to withhold assent (unlike US)
  • Can be re-elected (only President Rajendra Prasad elected twice)
  • Emergency powers — Art 352, 356, 360
SUPREME COURT

Judiciary

  • Supreme Court — Art 124; Original, Appellate, Advisory jurisdiction
  • Collegium system — CJI + 4 senior judges recommend appointments
  • Basic Structure — Kesavananda Bharati (1973)
  • Judicial Activism — PIL introduced by Justice P.N. Bhagwati
  • High Courts — Art 214; subordinate to SC
ELECTIONS

Election Commission

  • Art 324 — independent constitutional body
  • Chief Election Commissioner — security of tenure (like SC judge)
  • Model Code of Conduct — not statutory
  • EVM introduced in — Parur (Kerala) 1982 (trial); nationwide 2004
  • VVPAT — Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail
